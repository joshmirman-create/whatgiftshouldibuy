About This Site

What Gifts Should I Buy was built to solve the hardest gift-giving problem: the person you barely know.

Easy gifts are easy. You know what your best friend wants. You know what your partner needs. The hard ones are the people you see twice a year, the colleague whose last name you have to look up, the in-law who says "don't get me anything," the kid whose party is this weekend and all you know is they like something called Skibidi Toilet.

Most gift guides make this worse, not better. "Top 10 gifts for dads" tells you nothing about your dad. The internet rewards volume, so you get lists of things that are technically gifts rather than recommendations that actually fit anyone.

One real detail is enough

This site asks for one real thing you know about the person. Not their age range and gender — one actual detail about who they are. "He loves his boat." "She just started running." "They're obsessed with true crime podcasts." "He's been talking about building a deck for three years and never started."

That detail is the whole difference between a gift that feels thoughtful and a gift that feels like you grabbed something on the way over. We're built around it. Tell us one real thing and we'll find something specific.

What you get

Every recommendation includes the specific product name, why it fits this person in particular, what people who've bought it actually say, a price range, and an Amazon link. There's also a book recommendation matched to their specific interests — not their age bracket, their actual interests. And four alternatives going in different directions if the main pick isn't right.
